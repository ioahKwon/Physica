5. Key Differences
================================================================================

[ AddB vs SKEL ]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Aspect            | AddBiomechanics        | SKEL
------------------|------------------------|----------------------------
Shoulder          | acromial (acromion)    | scapula + humerus (separate)
Spine             | torso (single)         | lumbar + thorax (separate)
Forearm           | radius only            | ulna + radius (rotation)
Foot              | calcn + toes           | calcn + toes (same)
Neck/Head         | None                   | head (present)

- Current mapping: acromial -> humerus (shoulder-arm connection)
  * scapula is too medial (close to spine), doesn't match acromial position
  * humerus is the actual shoulder-arm connection point, better fit

[ AddB vs SMPL ]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Aspect            | AddBiomechanics        | SMPL
------------------|------------------------|----------------------------
Joint count       | 20                     | 24
Shoulder          | acromial               | Collar + Shoulder
Spine             | torso                  | spine1/2/3 (3 levels)
Foot structure    | talus + calcn + toes   | Ankle + Foot (simplified)
Hand structure    | radius + hand          | Wrist + Hand

- Biomechanical accuracy: AddB >> SMPL
- Graphics compatibility: SMPL >> AddB

[ SKEL vs SMPL ]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Aspect            | SKEL                   | SMPL
------------------|------------------------|----------------------------
DOF               | 46 (Euler)             | 72 (Axis-angle)
Shoulder          | scapula + humerus      | Collar + Shoulder
Spine             | lumbar + thorax        | spine1/2/3
Forearm rotation  | ulna + radius (yes)    | Not possible
Foot structure    | talus + calcn + toes   | Ankle + Foot

- Anatomical accuracy: SKEL >> SMPL
- Deep learning compatibility: SMPL >> SKEL
- Forearm rotation: Only SKEL can represent this

6. Shoulder Mapping Problem Resolution Summary
================================================================================

[Problem]
- AddB's acromial (acromion) doesn't directly correspond to any SKEL joint
- Initial acromial -> scapula mapping: Predicted shoulders too narrow

[Attempted Solutions]
1. acromial -> scapula: Failed (scapula is too close to spine)
2. Virtual shoulder (weighted avg of scapula + humerus): Failed (arm position wrong)
3. acromial -> humerus: Success! (MPJPE 26.3mm)

[Final Solution]
- Use acromial -> humerus mapping
- Humerus is the actual glenohumeral joint (shoulder-arm connection)
- Position matches better with AddB's acromial marker
